# Phase 1 (Weeks 1–2): Diagnostic

See also: [dsa-prep-plan.md](dsa-prep-plan.md) for the full 4-phase plan this is the first phase of.

Solve 3 timed Medium problems from *each* of 12 topics, closed-book, no hints, and log the result honestly — this replaces the vague feeling of "I'm not confident" with an actual list. Expect to fail or struggle on several; that's the point of this phase, not a bad sign.

## The rules for every diagnostic problem

- **25 minutes per problem, strict.** This is diagnostic, not real-interview simulation, so it's slightly more generous than the ~20 min you'd get live — but still enforced, so the signal is honest.
- **Closed-book**: no searching, no hints, no glancing at a similar problem first.
- **If you're stuck at 25 minutes**: stop the timer, mark it "Not Solved Independently," *then* look at the solution and understand it. Seeing the solution afterward doesn't retroactively count as solved — log it honestly.
- **Rate confidence immediately after finishing (or after reading the solution), 1–5**:
  - 1 = no idea where to start
  - 2 = recognized the topic but couldn't produce a working approach
  - 3 = got a brute-force but not the optimal/intended approach
  - 4 = solved correctly, but slower than ideal or with minor bugs
  - 5 = solved cleanly and confidently, within time

`Date | Topic | Problem | Difficulty | Time Limit | Actual Time Taken | Solved Independently (Y/N) | Approach Quality (Brute-force/Optimal) | Confidence (1–5) | Needs Phase 2 Revisit (Y/N)`

A ready-to-use version, pre-filled with all 36 problems (Topic, Problem, Difficulty, Time Limit) mapped to their day in the schedule above, is in [phase-1-diagnostic-tracker.csv](phase-1-diagnostic-tracker.csv) — open it in Excel/Google Sheets/Numbers and fill in the remaining columns as you go.

## How to read the results

For each topic, look at its 3 logged rows:

- **Mark the topic "needs Phase 2 revisit"** if 2 or more of its 3 problems scored confidence ≤3, or weren't solved independently.
- **If all 3 problems scored 4–5**, that topic can get light treatment in Phase 2 — just 1–2 refresher problems instead of the full 8–12 — so Phase 2 time concentrates on what the diagnostic actually found weak, not what you assumed going in.
